Mission

We aim to develop Poor Peter’s AntAlmanac into the ultimate planning aid for UCI Anteaters and to sustain its operations with continual maintenance of existing features, innovation of additional features, and updates of the newest trends.

Inspiration

The word, “almanac”, was supposedly derived from the greek word for calendar, “almenichiaká”. Almanacs were originally designed to provide farmers with planting dates and weather forecasts; over time they developed to include a grabbag of future projections, recurring events and astrological information. In writing Poor Richard’s Almanack, Benjamin Franklin even included jokes, aphorisms, puzzles, and demographics. For us, the almanac embodies the means of planning for the future. In using historical information on course enrollment and in providing multiple calendars and custom events, we hope to provide Anteaters with the power to plan their quarters with greater precision, flexibility and confidence.

Belief

Good software is alive. It is more than bloodless zeros and ones. Instead it is an organic process that grows, learns and evolves, to provide a service using code. Good software takes feedback from users, and improves the quality of the service it provides. Good software seeks to catch up on the latest edge and provide them to its users. Good software stretches itself to provide additional services to solve other relevant problems users may face. In accordance to this belief, we do not want to create the AntAlmanac only then to move on and forget, letting it stagnate. We will continually maintain the app, and the AntAlmanac will live on even after its makers graduate. That is why we want to develop the AntAlmanac into an organization that self-revitalizes through continual recruitments of new talent.

Future

In the foreseeable future, we want to expand our services into an even more comprehensive all-in-one solution to scheduling at UCI. We hope to provide enrollment and waitlist predictions with greater accuracy. We want to enable Anteaters to map out their four-year plans. We plan to grow our services on mobile platforms.
